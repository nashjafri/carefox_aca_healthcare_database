What are ACA plans?

The Affordable Care Act (ACA), also known as Obamacare, is a federal law enacted in March 2010. Its primary goal is to increase access to affordable health insurance for Americans, improve the quality of care, and reduce overall health care costs.

ACA plans refer to health insurance policies that comply with the standards set by this law. These plans are available through the Health Insurance Marketplace where individuals and families can compare and purchase health coverage.

Key Features of ACA Plans

Guaranteed Coverage
Insurers cannot deny you coverage due to pre-existing conditions.
Essential Health Benefits
Plans must include hospitalization, prescriptions, maternity, mental health, and preventive care.
Income-Based Subsidies
Many qualify for tax credits or cost-sharing reductions based on income and household size.
Open & Special Enrollment
Purchase plans during Open Enrollment or after life events like job loss, marriage, or relocation.

Source: Status of State Medicaid Expansion Decisions (Kaiser Family Foundation)

The Affordable Care Act’s Medicaid expansion expanded Medicaid coverage to nearly all adults with incomes up to 138% of the Federal Poverty Level ($21,597 for an individual in 2025) and provided states with an enhanced federal matching rate (FMAP) for their expansion populations. To date, 41 states (including DC) have adopted the Medicaid expansion and 10 states have not adopted the expansion. Current status for each state is based on KFF tracking and analysis of state expansion activity.

Key Insurance Terms

  • Benefit: The amount payable by the insurance company to a plan member for medical costs.
  • Coinsurance: Your share of the cost for services after your deductible is met, usually as a percentage.
  • Copayment: A flat fee (e.g., $10) you pay for specific services, with your insurer covering the rest.
  • Deductible: The amount you pay each year before your insurance starts covering expenses.
  • MOOP (Max Out-of-Pocket): The most you’ll pay in a year, after which the plan pays 100%.
  • Premium: The monthly amount you or your employer pay to keep your insurance coverage active.
  • Network: The list of doctors, hospitals, and facilities your insurance works with at discounted rates.
  • In-network provider: A provider contracted with your insurance plan, typically costing you less.
  • Out-of-network provider: A provider not contracted with your plan, typically more expensive.
  • Exclusion/Limitation: Services or conditions not covered under your health plan.
  • Individual Health Insurance: Insurance purchased personally, not through an employer.
  • Medicaid: A state-managed, income-based insurance program for low-income individuals.
  • Medicare: Federal insurance for people 65+ and some younger individuals with disabilities.

Data Source

This app uses the HIX Compare dataset, sponsored by the Robert Wood Johnson Foundation. It provides data on ACA-compliant plans from 2014 to 2025, including on- and off-market plans in all 50 states and D.C.

The dataset includes detailed plan attributes like premiums, deductibles, out-of-pocket limits, and covered benefits, empowering users to make informed insurance choices.
